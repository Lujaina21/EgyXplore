Ramses II

(1279 to 1213 BCE)

Ramses II, commonly known as Ramses the Great, stands as one of the most illustrious figures in the history of ancient Egypt. His reign, spanning an impressive 66 years, left an indelible mark on the New Kingdom era. From military prowess to monumental constructions, Ramses II's legacy continues to captivate scholars and enthusiasts alike.

Early Life:

Born around 1303 BCE, Ramses II ascended to the throne in his early twenties as the third pharaoh of the 19th dynasty. His birth name, Usermaatre Setepenre, translates to "The Maat of Ra is Powerful, Chosen of Ra."

Military Achievements:

Ramses II's military campaigns, notably the Battle of Kadesh against the Hittites, are etched into the annals of history. While the outcome of Kadesh remains debated, Ramses II strategically depicted himself as victorious on numerous monuments, showcasing both his military might and political acumen.
